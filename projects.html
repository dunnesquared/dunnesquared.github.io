Cinescoutüéû (v1.x)

My latest project!

Cinescout is a Flask-based, mobile-responsive website where you can learn more about any film or person in the world of cinema. All of Cinescout's data is fetched in real-time from The Movie Database and The New York Times.

With Cinescout you can

  • Read a brief synopsis of a film from The Movie Database, as well as a review summary from The New York Times.
  • Discover the filmography of your favourite cast or crew member.
  • Add movies to a personal list for later reference.
  • Browse through a list of critically-acclaimed films from The Criterion Collection.

Cinescout is available to try online. In the demo, you'll be able to search for films and crew members, but won't be able to create an account, maintain a movie list or see NYT movie reviews. For security and maintainability reasons, I've made myself the gatekeeper and limited these features to registered users. Click here if you're curious to know more.

That said, you can get some idea of what these features look like by watching my Razzie-winning screencast of Cinescout v1.0. It's a bit out of date UI-wise, but you'll get the picture. I hear watching the video works wonders for insomnia :-p.

Tech used:
Python 3.6.1, Flask, JavaScript ES6, requests, SQLAlchemy, Flask-Admin, Flask-Login, Flask-WTF, unittest, SQLite, Bootstrap 4.5, Bash, Ajax, DataTables, fuzzywuzzy

Sentence Cow is a Flask-based web tool that extracts sentences from a text, or at least tries to as best as possible (it's not a bad word- and character- counter either). It doesn't use any NLP algorithms to detect sentences‚Äìjust a lot regular expressions and condition-checking.
