About

As mentioned on the homepage, my name is Alex Dunne. I live in Montreal, Quebec, Canada, where I've been teaching and tutoring high-school math for almost a decade.

In 2019, I decided it was time to put my software engineering degree to good use. Ever since, I've been brushing up my coding skills to transition to a career in software development.

As a niche, I've decided to focus on Python web development for now, although I am open to opportunities in automation, data science and AI. I'm curious about where programming intersects mathematics, education, medicine, research, journalism, gaming, good causes and the arts. Data privacy and human-factors design interest me too.

At the end of the day, I hope for a software development career that is about more than making a decent living. Ideally, it would entail working with kind, responsible people to build something that meaningfully delights, edifies or helps others.

For more information on the professional side of things, please check out my LinkedIn profile and my GitHub account.
